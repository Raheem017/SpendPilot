# Go-To-Market Strategy: SpendPilot

### Exact Target User

The **Head of Engineering** or **VP of Finance** at Series A-B startups. Specifically, companies with 15–50 engineers who have recently scaled their use of AI agents (e.g., Cursor, Claude Code). These users are seeing their "Compute & Tooling" line item spike but lack the time to manually audit seat-level utility.

### The "Right Before" Moment

The user is scrolling **X (Twitter)** or **LinkedIn** and sees a peer complaining about a $5,000 "surprise" bill from OpenAI or Anthropic. They immediately check their own dashboard, feel a sense of dread, and look for a "benchmark" to see if their spend is normal.

### Online Hangouts

- **X (Twitter)**: Specific lists of "AI Founders" and "FinOps" influencers.
- **Discord**: The "LlamaIndex" and "LangChain" servers, specifically the #deployment and #general channels where token-cost optimization is a daily topic.
- **Subreddits**: r/LocalLLaMA (for those looking to switch to local to save) and r/SaaS.

### The First 100 Users ($0 Budget)

1.  **Cold DM Strategy**: Identify 50 founders on X who follow "SaaS Spend" accounts. Send a personalized link: "Saw you mention AI costs—built this 60s audit tool for Credex to help startups find leaks. No login req."
2.  **Product Hunt "Coming Soon"**: Leak the audit logic as a "Cheat Sheet" on LinkedIn to build a waitlist of 20–30 people before the Vercel link goes viral.
3.  **Community Value-Add**: Answer "How do I lower my API costs?" threads on StackOverflow and Reddit with a link to the tool as a free calculator.

### The Unfair Channel

**Credex VC Partnerships**: Credex can provide this tool to VC firms (e.g., YC, a16z) as a "Portfolio Spend Health Check." VCs mandate their startups use it to reduce burn, instantly funneling high-quality, high-spend leads directly to Credex consultations.

### Week-1 Traction Goals

- 500 Unique Visits.
- 150 Completed Audits (30% conversion).
- 15 Credex Consultation Bookings (10% of audited users).
